Alex Karp Biography And Wiki

Alex Karp is an American billionaire businessman from Palo Alto, California, US. He is co-founder and CEO of secretive Silicon Valley data miner Palantir Technologies, a software firm. He oversees the data-mining startup that received early backing from CIA investment arm In-Q-Tel. Being in a field dominated by MIT computer scientists and Harvard MBAs, Karp actually doesn’t hail from either discipline.
He managed money before starting Palantir in 2004. He also occasionally teaches meditation classes at the company.

Alex Karp Background, Education, PhD

Alex attended Central High School from where he graduated in 1985. He earned a bachelor’s degree from Haverford College. He later enrolled at Stanford University earning a J.D. and a doctorate in neoclassical social theory from Frankfurt University.

Alex Karp CEO Palantir Technologies, Peter Thiel, Caedmon – Career

Karp began his business career in his first venture Caedmon Group. It is an asset management company based in London, which resulted in a successful project.

Alex met Palantir cofounder and billionaire Facebook investor and Paypal co-founder Peter Thiel while at Stanford Law School. Thiel states that the idea for the company was based on the realization that “the approaches that PayPal had used to fight fraud could be extended into other contexts, like fighting terrorism.”

In its initial stage, Palantir’s only backers were the Central Intelligence Agency’s venture capital arm In-Q-Tel. It later grew with Thiel being the company’s largest shareholder. Palantir raised $880 million in venture funding that in turn valued the company at $20 billion.

The company does contract work for government agencies like the NYPD and is reportedly the largest holder of real estate in Palo Alto.

Alex Karp Writing

Karp has written a thesis “Aggression in der Lebenswelt: Die Erweiterung des Parsonsschen Konzepts der Aggression Durch die Beschreibung des Zusammenhangs von Jargon, Aggression und Kultur”. It was supervised by Karola Brede. The thesis means “Aggression in the life-world: The extension of Parsons’ concept of aggression by describing the connection between jargon, aggression, and culture.”
