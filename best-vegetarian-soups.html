Best Vegetarian Soups: Best Vegetarian Canned Soup You Can Buy

July 2024 · 4 minute read

The best vegetarian soups are available to you whenever you want—because they come in cans! No longer will you have to slave over homemade vegetable stock to experience true soupy bliss. We tasted some of the top rated vegetarian canned soup brands on the market and found the best ones you can buy. 

It doesn’t matter whether you’re making soup with an entire chicken or some root vegetables, good soup should be well seasoned, comforting, and substantial enough to be a meal on its own. It should also have a great texture—whether that’s totally smooth or studded with fun food bits. The best vegetarian soup needs to do all of that with no help from the animal kingdom. 

In one of our most wholesome taste tests yet, I sat down to 13 different canned vegetarian soups in order to find the best vegetarian soup that you can buy at the grocery store. Here are the six that I think deserve a spot in your pantry. 

Best Fake Meat

Gardein Plant-Based Saus’ge GumboInstacart

This gumbo isn’t just vegetarian, it’s straight-up vegan canned soup. Made with okra, red bell peppers, celery, white rice, Cajun spices, and something called plant-based saus’ge, it’s a good vegan approximation of a gumbo. It’s hearty thanks to the rice (fun fact about me: I love rice in soup) and the big chunks of saus’ge are spiced with a good peppery flavor. “If you are a vegan, this will be impressive,” former New Orleanian and Sporked senior writer Jordan Myrick said. “But it’s not for meat eaters.” As a meat eater, I agree, I would not pick this over a meaty gumbo. But I think vegans deserve sausage gumbo, too. And this one will do nicely.

Best Creamy

Pacific Foods Organic Thai Sweet Potato SoupTarget

Some of the best vegetarian soups are filled with tons of vegetables, and some require no chewing at all. This is the latter kind of vegetarian soup. There are some chunks of sweet potato but they’re very soft. It’s a very comforting soup and if you paired it with a big hunk of toasted sourdough, you’d have a great lunch for a rainy day. There’s tons of lemongrass aroma and nice curry flavors. “It feels healthy,” said Sporked managing editor Gwynedd Stuart. But it’s still creamy, too.

Credit: Merc / Target

Rating:

7.5/10

Best Vegetable Soup

Campbell’s Well Yes! Garden Vegetable Soup With PastaTarget

This is a vegetable-forward vegetarian soup. The broth is light and herbaceous and, very importantly, the pasta isn’t mushy. I hate mushy pasta in canned soup. There’s lots of roasted red pepper and tomato flavor, it’s seasoned with pepper and not too much salt. I’d eat this hearty canned vegetarian soup out of a mug, curled up on the couch. It’s the best vegetarian soup when you really just want some vegetables in soup.

Credit: Merc / Target

Rating:

8/10

Sporks

Best Restaurant Quality

Amy’s Kitchen Thai Coconut SoupTarget

This take on tom kha phak is so creamy and delicious. Made with sweet coconut milk, lime leaf, lemongrass, tofu, shiitake mushrooms, sweet potatoes, and green beans, it’s a vegan canned soup that even non-vegans will love. While it’s perfectly satisfying as is, you could add some rice (maybe leftover from last night’s dinner), as well as a squeeze of lime and a drizzle of hot sauce for a restaurant-quality feast.

Credit: Merc / Target

Rating:

8.5/10

Best Almost Homemade

Maya Kaimal Creamy Spiced Butternut SoupThrive Market

“Yum yum yum,” I wrote in my notes. This boxed soup (it still counts as canned in my book!) is so flavorful. It’s sweet but not overly so, balanced out with some acidity. Gwynedd favorably compared this to her own homemade soups, while Jordan said it was reminiscent of rasam. Try pairing this creamy vegetarian soup with a grilled cheese sandwich for a real game-changing lunch. 

Credit: Merc / Thrive Market

Rating:

9/10

Sporks

Best of the Best

Amy’s Quinoa, Kale & Red LentilTarget

For me, the best vegetarian soups give me something new and interesting. And this one certainly does that with its mix of quinoa, kale, and lentil. There’s lots of turmeric flavor and tons of fun textures, so every spoonful is different. And there’s no stringy kale! Just good, in-tact kale leaves. This is lunch. This is a substantial lunch. I will be buying this again, because it’s simply the best vegetarian soup I’ve tried.

Credit: Merc / Target

Rating:

9.5/10
