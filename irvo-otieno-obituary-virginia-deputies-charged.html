Irvo Otieno Obituary is trending around the Internet as Irvo tragically died in police custody, and a viral video shows deputies piling on top of him.

Irvo Otieno, aka Young Vo, was an aspiring rapper who streamed his music online using titles like “Man in the Middle,” “Can’t Wait,” “Trapped Out,” and “Frenzy.”

According to his mother, Caroline Ouko, Otieno was 28, able to write a song in under five minutes, and ambitious to create his record label.

She described him as a creative and intelligent young man who had found his passion in music.

However, Otieno, whose family came from Kenya when he was four years old, died on March 6 at Central State Hospital in Virginia,

The reason for his death was asphyxiation caused by being restrained during a mental health crisis.

Irvo’s mother said- “My son was treated like a dog. Worse than a dog. My son was tortured” when she saw the video.

Seven Henrico County sheriff’s deputies and three hospital security guards have been charged with second-degree murder concerning his death.

Irvo Otieno Obituary: Death Video Reddit

A video that has gone viral on social media, including Reddit, shows brutal behavior toward a man named Irvo Otieno.

Social media is swarmed with the obituary for Otieno and rage for the police officers who mistreated Irvo till he died.

Irvo was brought into the admission room of Central State Hospital in Dinwiddie County, Virginia, on March 6.

In the silent video, Otieno is handcuffed and in leg irons before being led to a chair.

Roughly eight minutes later, two officers are shown holding him on the ground and reacting when he moves.

They eventually lose their grip on him, causing him to roll across the floor, and afterward, more officers and hospital staff arrive to restrain him.

Eventually, at least nine people are seen piling on top of him to hold him down while other hospital staff looks on. The video shows that he was held down for 11 minutes.

The Washington Post obtained the video by accessing Dropbox links included in a public court filing.

Irvo Died In Police Custody, And Virginia Deputies Charged

Otieno was immobile 20 minutes after entering the admissions room, and despite resuscitation efforts lasting less than an hour, he died at the scene.

The preliminary report from the Office of the Chief Medical Examiner has identified asphyxiation as the cause of death.

The Washington Post obtained a video of Otieno being restrained, and attorney Ben Crump, representing Otieno’s family, has criticized the brutal nature of the restraint.

Defense attorneys did their best to block the video from being released, but prosecutor Ann Cabell Baskervill has stated that she plans to make it public on Tuesday.

Seven Virginia sheriff’s deputies and three hospital employees have been charged with second-degree murder concerning Otieno’s death.

The grand jury in Dinwiddie County is set to meet on Tuesday to determine the final charges.
